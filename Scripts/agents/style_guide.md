# Production Style Guide

## Animation
- **Style:** Flat illustration, slightly textured. Think illustrated history book that learned to move.
- **Characters:** Expressive but not cartoonish. Simple shapes, strong silhouettes. Readable at small size. Not stick figures — closer to Kurzgesagt warmth with slightly rougher, hand-drawn edges.
- **Maps:** Simple, slightly stylized world map used throughout. Countries and regions label themselves as they become relevant. Borders appear and disappear like breathing.

## Color Palette
| Era | Palette |
|---|---|
| Ancient / Viking age | Warm amber, burnt sienna, deep ochre |
| Medieval | Muted greens, stone grey, candlelight gold |
| Colonial / suppression periods | Cold grey-blue, desaturated |
| Revolution / turning points | Deep red accents |
| Modern era | Clean Nordic white, sky blue, warm neutral |
| 2008 / financial collapse | Red. Everything going red. |

## Typography (on-screen labels)
- Clean sans-serif for dates and labels
- Never cluttered — one label at a time
- Dates appear in the corner, not centre-screen

## Characters
Each major figure gets a consistent design used every time they appear. Key design principle: **silhouette must be instantly recognisable.** Viewer should know who it is before the label appears.

### General rules
- Costume reflects era and role, not caricature
- Expression tells you the personality before the narrator says a word
- Recurring characters should feel like people, not symbols

## Music
- **Style:** Original orchestral-folk hybrid. Country-specific instrumentation where relevant (e.g. Icelandic strings for Iceland episodes).
- **Rule:** No comedy music stings. Let the words carry the lightness. Music sets emotional tone, not punchlines.
- **Transitions:** Musical tone shifts with era — do not keep the same cue running across a major historical turning point.

| Moment type | Music note |
|---|---|
| Cold open | Minimal, tense. Single instrument or silence. |
| Rewind/time jump | Short musical reset — not a whoosh, a breath |
| Catastrophe (plague, famine, eruption) | Low, slow, heavy. Let it sit. |
| Victory / independence | Rising, clear — not triumphant fanfare, just: resolved |
| Modern close | Wide, quiet. Return to opening theme. |

## Narration
- **Voice:** Single narrator. Warm, measured, slightly dry. Not breathless, not theatrical.
- **Pace:** ~140 words per minute natural conversational pace
- **Pauses:** Written into the script with "..." — honour them in recording
- **Tone:** Never condescending. Never mocking the subject country.

## Runtime Targets
| Format | Narration words | Total video length |
|---|---|---|
| Standard episode | 1,800–2,000 words | 14–16 min |
| Extended episode | 2,200–2,500 words | 17–20 min |
| Two-part episode (each part) | 1,800–2,200 words | 14–18 min each |
