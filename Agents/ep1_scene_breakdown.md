## Table 2: Music Brief

| Segment | Tone | Instrumentation | Key rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cold Open (Scenes 1–8) | Tense, spare, unsettling | Single low bass pulse; near silence; one sustained sub-bass tone | No melody — rhythm only; music must not tip into thriller; let the visuals and numbers carry the dread; one pulse then silence until the time rewind |
| Before the Vikings / Papar Monks (Scenes 9–13) | Remote, peaceful, slightly eerie | Solo Icelandic fiddle (langspil or violin) playing a high, sparse modal melody; no percussion | Must feel genuinely peaceful, not comedic; the monk's serenity is real; music shifts the instant the longship appears — a single harmonic dissonance, not a sting |
| Settlement / Founding (Scenes 14–25) | Purposeful, warm, slightly absurd | Sparse Icelandic strings, moderate tempo; occasional pizzicato for the pillar comedy beats | Never triumphant — Ingólfr is stubborn and a little ridiculous; the music should feel like a man who is absolutely committed to a questionable plan |
| The Great Migration (Scenes 21–25) | Expansive, quiet, settling | Strings widen slightly; low sustained cello beneath the melody; sparse texture | Music should feel like water finding its level — unhurried, inevitable; no march rhythm; it is not heroic, it is human-scale |
| Althing Established (Scenes 26–31) | Deliberate, open, slightly bright | Strings brighten a half-step; add a single wind instrument (recorder or low flute) under the melody; still sparse | Must not feel triumphant or democratic-fanfare; this is people talking in a field, not a state ceremony; the brightness is subtle |
| Year 1000 / Conversion (Scenes 32–37) | Suspended, uncertain, then quietly resolved | Strings thin down to one or two voices; held notes; micro-pauses; resolves to a single quiet chord at compromise | Music must pause entirely when Þorgeir goes under the cloak — silence under the time-lapse; the resolution chord is understated, not relieved |
| Erik / Leif / Exploration (Scenes 38–47) | Wry, slightly wistful, light | Single plucked string instrument; gentle pizzicato; no sustained melody; a slight minor-modal tinge on the Newfoundland departure | The exploration music should feel light and slightly amused — not adventure music; Leif's departure cue is the single mournful string note described in the script; hold it |
| Saga Age / Snorri Writing (Scenes 48–54) | Warm, intellectual, candlelit | Return of strings in a new configuration — slower, richer, candlelight gold feel; the melody is the cold open theme slowed and ornamented | Snorri's scenes should feel like being inside a warm room late at night; when his attention shifts, the music cools by a half-step without changing tempo |
| Sturlung Age (Scenes 55–65) | Heavy, cold, grinding | Low strings only; no melody — sustained dissonant drones; occasional pizzicato like distant percussion; tempo slows | Must never become action-music or drama-score; it is the sound of exhaustion, not battle; music stops completely at "Eigi skal höggva" — complete silence for the words and for "They struck" |
| Old Covenant / Surrender (Scenes 66–71) | Resigned, low, still | Single low cello sustain; under the narration only; no harmony | This is not sad-music — do not play grief; it is the sound of things going underground; the final Þingvellir image gets the single low note that pulses once |
| Close Montage (Scenes 72–75) | Building, layered, then resolved | Near-silence under the rapid montage narration; then as the roots-showing sequence arrives, the opening cold open theme returns — wider, fuller, strings now supported by low winds | The cold open theme return must not feel triumphant; it feels like recognition — something has persisted; it resolves without fanfare on the final wide island shot |
| **GLOBAL RULES** | | | (1) No comedy music stings — tone is always carried by words, never by a musical joke. (2) Music shifts with each major era — never the same configuration for two consecutive segments. (3) The cold open bass pulse and the final string theme are the same musical DNA — the close should feel like the open finally breathed. |
