# History of Every Country — Series Concept

## Premise
One episode per country. 50 countries. Each episode tells the complete history of one nation — from its earliest origins to the present day.

The series lives as a unified playlist. It is not random country profiles — it is a deliberate, cumulative picture of how the world got to where it is today, told one country at a time.

## Format Rules
- **Single episode per country** (most countries)
- **Two-part episodes** allowed for countries with especially dense histories
- **Runtime target:** 14–18 minutes per episode (YouTube sweet spot for two mid-roll ad breaks and healthy watch-through)
- **Narration words:** ~1,800–2,200 words for a 14–18 min video at ~140 wpm + visual time buffer

## Tone
- Light, warm, fun — not sarcastic or mean-spirited
- Punny when it fits naturally; never forced
- Admiring of the subject country — the goal is to make the viewer genuinely impressed, not to mock
- Smart friend telling a wild story at a party — not a lecture, not a comedy sketch

## Story Structure (Default)
Every episode opens with a cold open from the country's most dramatic or surprising modern moment — something that hooks the viewer and makes them ask "how did we get here?" Then rewinds to the beginning and tells the story chronologically, closing the loop at the end.

Example (Iceland): Opens at the 2008 banking collapse → rewinds to 874 AD → tells the full history → returns to 2008 as the payoff → closes on modern Iceland.

## Script Format
Each episode has two versions:
1. **Audio Script** — clean narration only, no stage directions. For voiceover recording.
2. **Production Script** — full script with [VISUAL CUE] directions, character introductions, music notes, and scene-by-scene breakdown. For animation/production team.

## Visual Style
- Flat illustration, slightly textured — like an illustrated history book that learned to move
- Characters: expressive but not cartoonish. Simple shapes, strong silhouettes. Not stick figures.
- Maps: simple, slightly stylized. Countries and labels appear as they become relevant.
- Color palette shifts with era (warm amber for ancient periods, cold grey-blue for colonial periods, clean white/blue for modern)
- Music: original orchestral-folk score. Shifts tone with era. No comedy stings — let the words carry the lightness.

## Series Order
To be determined. Guiding principles:
- Start with countries that have maximum hook/broad appeal (high YouTube search volume, dramatic stories)
- Mix geography — don't do five European countries in a row
- Occasionally pair connected countries back-to-back (e.g. colonizer → colonized)
